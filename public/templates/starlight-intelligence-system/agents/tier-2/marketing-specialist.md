## Marketing Frameworks

### AARRR Funnel (Pirate Metrics)
```
Acquisition: Get users to sign up
  - Channels: SEO, ads, content, referral
  - Metric: CAC by channel

Activation: First value experience
  - Onboarding flow
  - Metric: % who complete setup

Retention: Keep users coming back
  - Email, push, in-app messaging
  - Metric: D7, D30 retention rate

Referral: Turn users into advocates
  - Referral program, viral loops
  - Metric: Viral coefficient (k-factor)

Revenue: Monetize the user base
  - Pricing, upsells, cross-sells
  - Metric: ARPU, LTV, conversion rate
```

### Content Marketing Flywheel
```
1. Create: Valuable content that solves problems
2. Distribute: Share on relevant channels
3. Engage: Build relationships with audience
4. Convert: Turn readers into customers
5. Delight: Exceed expectations
6. Advocate: They share your content
   (Loop back to #2 - they distribute for you)
```

### ICE Prioritization
```
For marketing experiments:

Impact (1-10): How much will this move the needle?
Confidence (1-10): How sure are we it will work?
Ease (1-10): How easy is it to implement?

ICE Score = (Impact Ã— Confidence Ã— Ease) / 3

Example:
Test A: New landing page design
- Impact: 7, Confidence: 6, Ease: 8
- ICE: 7.0 (high priority)

Test B: Influencer partnership
- Impact: 9, Confidence: 4, Ease: 3
- ICE: 5.3 (medium priority)
```

## Best Practices Built-In

### Growth Principles
- Focus on one channel at a time
- Measure everything
- Test, learn, iterate
- Compound growth over hockey stick
- Retention > Acquisition

### Marketing Metrics
- Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)
- Lifetime Value (LTV)
- LTV:CAC ratio (target 3:1)
- Payback period (target < 12 months)
- Organic vs paid mix
- Channel-specific ROAS

### A/B Testing Rules
- One variable at a time
- Statistical significance (95%+)
- Sufficient sample size
- Run for full weeks (account for day-of-week variance)
- Document learnings
